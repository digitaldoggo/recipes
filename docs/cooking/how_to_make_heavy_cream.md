# How To Make Heavy Cream (Substitute)

A 2-ingredient hack for making heavy cream when you're out — melt butter into whole milk, blend, and you've got a rich creamy liquid.

**Source:** [Preppy Kitchen](https://preppykitchen.com/how-to-make-heavy-cream/)

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| ---------- | -------- |
| Unsalted Butter | 6 tablespoons (84g) |
| Whole Milk | ½ cup (120mL) |

**Yields:** ~1 cup · **Prep:** 5 min · **Cook:** 2 min · **Chill:** 1–3 hours

## Directions

1. Cut butter into tablespoon-sized pieces and place in a small saucepan with the milk.
2. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, for about 4 minutes until butter melts completely. Remove from heat.
3. Pour mixture into a blender. Blend on high speed for **1–2 minutes** (this emulsifies it).
4. Use immediately or refrigerate in an airtight container for up to **4 hours** before using (it thickens as it chills).

## Important Notes

- **Does NOT whip.** This substitute won't hold peaks — use only where liquid heavy cream is needed, not for whipped cream.
- **Use unsalted butter.** Salted butter will make your dish salty and throw off the flavor balance.
- **Must be whole milk.** Skim or plant-based milks don't have enough fat to emulsify properly.
- **Chill time matters.** The mixture thickens and best resembles heavy cream after 1–3 hours in the fridge.

## Storage

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Fridge | Airtight container, up to 4 days |
| If separated | Shake or re-blend until creamy again |

## How To Use It

**Savory:** Creamy soups, casseroles, quiche, creamed spinach, potato soup, vodka sauce, alfredo sauce, chicken pot pie

**Sweet:** Chocolate ganache, bread pudding, caramel sauce, scones, banana pudding, buttercream and other frostings, baked goods

**Drinks:** Mudslide, brandy Alexander, coffee drinks
