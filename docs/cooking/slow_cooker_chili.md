# Slow Cooker Chili

Hearty, cozy, and incredibly easy — just brown the beef, toss everything in the slow cooker, and let it do its thing. Great for meal prep and even better the next day.

**Source:** [Preppy Kitchen](https://preppykitchen.com/slow-cooker-chili/)

## Ingredients

| Ingredient              | Quantity       |
| ----------------------- | -------------- |
| Ground Beef (90/10 or 85/15) | 2 lbs (900g) |
| Onion, chopped          | 1 large        |
| Green Bell Pepper, diced| 1              |
| Garlic cloves, minced   | 4              |
| Kidney Beans            | 3 × 15.5 oz cans, drained & rinsed |
| Diced Tomatoes          | 2 × 14.5 oz cans (undrained) |
| Tomato Sauce            | 1 × 28 oz can  |
| Beef Broth              | ½ cup (120 mL) |
| Chili Powder            | 2 Tbsp         |
| Ground Cumin            | 2 tsp          |
| Dried Oregano           | 1½ tsp         |
| Salt                    | 1½ tsp         |
| Black Pepper            | ½ tsp          |

**Servings:** 8 · **Prep:** 15 min · **Cook:** 3–4h high / 6–8h low

## Directions

1. **Brown the meat** — In a large skillet over medium-high heat, cook beef, onion, bell pepper, and garlic until beef is browned (~6 minutes). Drain excess fat if needed.
2. **Combine everything** — Transfer beef mixture to a 6-quart slow cooker. Add beans, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, broth, chili powder, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
3. **Cook** — Cover and cook on **high for 3–4 hours** or **low for 6–8 hours**. No need to stir during cooking, but you can if desired. The end result should be thick, not soupy.
4. **Serve** with your favorite toppings.

## Secret Ingredients (pick 2–3)

These pantry staples take the chili from great to unforgettable:

- **Cocoa powder** — 2 tsp unsweetened; deepens flavor and enriches color
- **Cinnamon** — pinch to ¼ tsp for warmth (not cinnamon-y, just enhances other flavors)
- **Sweetener** — 1 Tbsp brown sugar or a glug of maple syrup
- **Vinegar** — 1 Tbsp apple cider or red wine vinegar for brightness
- **Soy / Worcestershire sauce** — swap salt for umami depth
- **Mustard** — 1 Tbsp Dijon or spicy brown for tang and kick
- **Beer** — swap beef broth for lager (richer with milk stout)
- **Hot sauce** — a few dashes for heat
- **Seasoning mix** — ranch powder or French onion soup mix

## Pro Tips

- **Keep the lid on.** Long cooking without it dries out the top and makes chili too thick.
- **Brown the meat first.** Raw beef in the slow cooker steams — browning kicks off the Maillard reaction for way more savory flavor. (Steamed vs chargrilled hamburger, you know which wins.)
- **Make ahead.** Brown the beef and veggies, cool, refrigerate in a container. Dump everything into the slow cooker when ready to cook.
- **Instant Pot?** Use the sauté function to brown meat, then switch to slow cooker mode. Don't pressure cook — this recipe is designed for low-and-slow.

## How To Serve

**Toppings:** Shredded cheese, sour cream, sliced green onions, pickled red onions, fried onions, guacamole

**Sides:** Cornbread, tortilla chips, cheddar biscuits, garlic bread

**Leftover repurposing:** Nachos, chili dogs/burgers, baked potatoes, tater tots with cheese (chili cheese fries!), stuffed peppers

## Storage & Freezing

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Fridge | Airtight container, up to 5 days |
| Freeze | Freezer-safe container, up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in fridge before reheating. |
| Reheat | Stovetop, microwave, or slow cooker on Low for ~2 hours |
