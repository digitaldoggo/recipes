# Baked Salmon with Dill

**Source:** https://tastesbetterfromscratch.com/favorite-baked-salmon-with-dill/

## Ingredients

- 1½ – 2 lbs salmon fillet (or about six 4oz portions)
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

### Garlic Butter
- 3 Tablespoons butter, melted
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice
- Fresh chopped parsley and lemon slices for garnish (optional)

## Instructions

1. Rest salmon on the counter for 20–30 minutes to come to room temperature. Pat dry with paper towels. Line a sheet pan or baking tray with aluminum foil and lightly spray with non-stick cooking spray. Place fillet skin side down.
2. **Garlic butter:** Melt butter and stir in garlic and lemon juice. Spoon mixture over the flesh of the salmon fillet. (You can enclose the foil around the salmon if you prefer it more moist, but it's not required.)
3. Bake at 400°F for 12–15 minutes depending on thickness and desired doneness. Aim for 120–130°F for medium rare to medium (USDA recommends 145°F). Remove a few degrees before target as it will continue cooking while resting. Check by gently pulling back on the thickest part with a fork — it should flake easily.

## Tips

- **Salmon types:** King (Chinook), Silver (Coho), Red (Sockeye), Pink, or Atlantic salmon all work well.
- **Freshness check:** Fresh salmon should have a mild salt-water smell (not fishy), spring back when gently poked, and have a bright orange-red hue.
- **Thawing:** Thaw frozen salmon in the refrigerator or under cold running water — never hot water.
- **Don't rinse:** Washing fish contaminates your sink and can break down the flesh. Just pat dry.
- **Season right before baking:** Adding salt too early draws out moisture and dries the salmon.
- **Leave skin on** during baking — it protects the fillet and slides off easily when serving.
- **Don't overcook.** Restaurants typically serve salmon medium rare. Remove just before done; carryover cooking will finish it.

## Variations

- **Cajun:** Brush with olive oil, sprinkle generously with Cajun seasoning, bake as directed.
- **Dill:** Season with garlic butter as above, then sprinkle fresh chopped dill on top after baking (or dried dill before).
- **Air Fryer:** Cook at 400°F for 8–10 minutes.

## Serve With

Green Beans, Potatoes Au Gratin, Whole Roasted Cauliflower, Waldorf Salad, Vinegar Coleslaw, or Beet Salad. Leftover salmon is great on salads or in salmon bowls.
