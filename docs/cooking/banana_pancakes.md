# Banana Pancakes

Soft, fluffy pancakes sweetened naturally by ripe bananas. Make-ahead and freezer-friendly — perfect for quick weekday breakfasts or lazy weekends.

**Source:** [Natasha's Kitchen](https://natashaskitchen.com/banana-pancakes/)

## Ingredients

| Ingredient              | Quantity       |
| ----------------------- | -------------- |
| Buttermilk (or kefir)   | 1¼ cups        |
| All-Purpose Flour       | 1½ cups, measured correctly |
| Granulated Sugar        | 1 Tbsp (optional) |
| Baking Soda             | 1 tsp          |
| Salt                    | ¼ tsp          |
| Unsalted Butter (melted)| 3 Tbsp         |
| Eggs                    | 2 large        |
| Vanilla Extract         | 1 tsp          |
| Bananas, mashed         | 2 medium (~1 cup) |
| Olive Oil               | 2 Tbsp, divided (for sautéing) |

**Servings:** ~12 pancakes

## Directions

1. **Combine dry ingredients** — In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking soda, and salt. Make a well in the center.
2. **Mash bananas** with a fork until chunky applesauce consistency.
3. **Combine wet ingredients** — In a second bowl, whisk together buttermilk, eggs, vanilla, mashed bananas, and melted butter.
4. **Make the batter** — Pour wet into dry and mix just until combined. Lumps are fine — don't overmix.
5. **Heat skillet** — Add 1 Tbsp olive oil to a cast-iron or non-stick skillet over medium heat.
6. **Scoop batter** — Use ¼ cup portions onto the greased skillet.
7. **Cook first side** — About 2 minutes, until edges look matte and large bubbles form on top and start to pop.
8. **Flip and cook** — Another ~2 minutes until golden brown.

## Tips

- **Ripe bananas are key.** Browner = sweeter pancakes. Yellow bananas work but won't be as flavorful.
- **Don't overmix.** Stop when the last streaks of flour disappear — overmixed batter = dense pancakes.
- **Make-ahead friendly.** Mix the batter and refrigerate overnight; cook straight from the fridge in the morning.
- **Freezer-friendly.** Reheat frozen pancakes in an air fryer, skillet, or toaster.

## Toppings (favorites)

- Sliced bananas & maple syrup
- Toasted pecans or walnuts
- Peanut butter / almond butter / Nutella
- Fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries)
- Honey butter or butterscotch sauce
- Chocolate chips

## Storage & Freezing

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Room temp | Airtight container, up to 2 days |
| Fridge | Up to 4 days |
| Freeze | Wrap individually in plastic + foil, freezer bag, up to 3 months. Reheat in air fryer, skillet, or toaster. |

## Variations

- **Banana nut:** Fold in ½–1 cup toasted walnuts or pecans
- **Chocolate chip:** Add ½–1 cup chocolate chips to batter
- **Protein boost:** Swap some flour for protein powder (add extra buttermilk)
- **No buttermilk?** Mix 1¼ cups milk + 1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice, let sit 5 min
