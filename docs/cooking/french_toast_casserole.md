# French Toast Casserole

A hands-off breakfast casserole with a crisp cinnamon sugar top and soft, custard-soaked bread inside. Perfect for brunch, holidays, or feeding a crowd.

**Source:** [Preppy Kitchen](https://preppykitchen.com/french-toast-casserole/)

## Ingredients

### Filling

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| ---------- | -------- |
| Large Eggs | 6 |
| Whole Milk | 1¾ cups (370mL) |
| Light Brown Sugar (packed) | ¼ cup (55g) |
| Vanilla Extract | 1½ teaspoons |
| Ground Cinnamon | ¾ teaspoon |
| Salt | ¼ teaspoon |
| French Bread, cut into 1-inch cubes | 1 loaf (~14–16 oz) |

### Topping

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| ---------- | -------- |
| Light Brown Sugar (packed) | ¼ cup (55g) |
| Ground Cinnamon | ½ teaspoon |
| Salt | ¼ teaspoon |
| Cold Butter, diced | ¼ cup (57g) |

**Servings:** 8–10 · **Prep:** 15 min · **Cook:** 30 min · **Total:** 45 min

## Directions

1. Preheat oven to **375°F**. Lightly grease a 13×9-inch baking dish with butter.
2. Cut bread into 1-inch cubes (slightly stale bread works best).
3. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, brown sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt.
4. Stir in bread cubes until fully coated. Spoon into the prepared baking dish.
5. In a small bowl, whisk together topping: brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Sprinkle evenly over the bread.
6. Dot the top with diced cold butter.
7. Bake ~30 minutes until filling is set and top is golden brown.
8. Cool 5 minutes before serving. Serve with powdered sugar and maple syrup.

## Bread Tips

- **Best breads:** French bread, brioche, challah, or sourdough — sturdy with a slightly dense crumb.
- **Slightly stale = key.** Stale bread absorbs custard without getting soggy. Fresh bread? Cube it and toast in the oven for a few minutes first.
- **Avoid** very soft sliced sandwich bread — it turns mushy.

## Make-Ahead & Freezing

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Overnight | Assemble minus topping, cover, refrigerate up to 24h. Add topping and bake next morning. Great for Christmas! |
| Freeze (unbaked) | Assemble in disposable pan (minus topping), wrap tightly in plastic + foil, freeze up to 3 months. Thaw 24h in fridge before adding topping and baking. |
| Freeze (baked) | Cool completely, wrap well in plastic + foil, freeze up to 3 months. Thaw 24h in fridge, reheat at 350°F until warm. |

## Variations & Tips

- **Richer custard:** Swap half the milk for half-and-half, or use 1 cup milk + ¾ cup heavy cream.
- **Add fruit:** Toss 1 cup berries (strawberries, raspberries, blackberries, blueberries), diced apples, or chopped peaches on top of bread before adding topping.
- **Seasonal spices:** Swap cinnamon for pumpkin pie spice or apple pie spice.
- **Crunchy topping:** Add ½ cup chopped pecans or walnuts to the topping.
- **Streusel instead:** Skip cinnamon sugar and use a crisp streusel topping.
- **Browns too fast?** Tent with aluminum foil partway through baking.

## How To Reheat

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Oven (large portions) | 350°F until warmed through (~10 min) |
| Microwave (small portions) | ~1 minute |

## Storage

Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the fridge for **3–4 days**. Best enjoyed fresh from the oven.

## What To Serve With It

- Powdered sugar dusting
- Whipped cream + fresh berries
- Jam or preserves
- Maple syrup, caramel sauce, or vanilla sauce
- Crispy bacon or candied bacon
- Scrambled eggs and hash browns
