# Thai Peanut Chicken (Peanut Butter Stir Fry Chicken)

## Ingredients

| Ingredient               | Quantity   |
| ------------------------ | ---------- |
| Cooking oil              | 2 tablespoons |
| Garlic (minced)          | 3 cloves   |
| White onion (sliced)     | 1 medium   |
| Chicken breast           | 1 pound    |
| Peanut butter powder     | 3 tablespoons |
| Water                    | 1 tablespoon |
| Brown sugar              | 2 tablespoons |
| Soy sauce                | 2 tablespoons |
| Garlic chili sauce or sriracha | 1 teaspoon |

## Directions

1. Heat a large skillet over high heat. Add oil to the skillet.
2. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, then remove and set aside (it will burn if left in too long).
3. Add the onions and chicken to the skillet, turning the chicken every 2–3 minutes until cooked through.
4. While the chicken cooks, combine peanut butter powder, water, brown sugar, soy sauce, garlic chili sauce or sriracha, and the reserved garlic. Stir until smooth.
5. When the chicken is done, drizzle the sauce over it and cook for about 1 minute.
6. Remove from heat and serve over noodles or rice. Garnish with fresh cilantro and chopped peanuts if desired.

## Notes

- For less fat, use powdered peanut butter instead of regular.
- Great with added veggies like shredded carrots, celery, red bell pepper, broccoli, or bok choy.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for 4–5 days, or freeze for up to 4 months (freeze chicken separately from rice/noodles).
