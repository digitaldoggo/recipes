# Banana Muffins

Soft and moist banana muffins with a bakery-style domed top. Loaded with banana flavor and lightly spiced with cinnamon.

**Source:** [Preppy Kitchen](https://preppykitchen.com/banana-muffins/)

## Ingredients

| Ingredient              | Quantity       |
| ----------------------- | -------------- |
| All-Purpose Flour       | 1½ cups (180g) |
| Baking Powder           | 1 teaspoon     |
| Baking Soda             | 1 teaspoon     |
| Ground Cinnamon         | 1 teaspoon     |
| Salt                    | ¾ teaspoon     |
| Mashed Overripe Bananas | 1½ cups (320g, ~3 bananas) |
| Unsalted Butter (melted)| ½ cup (113g/120ml) |
| Granulated Sugar        | ½ cup (100g)   |
| Light Brown Sugar       | ¼ cup packed (55g) |
| Eggs                    | 2 large, room temp |
| Vanilla Extract         | 1 teaspoon     |

## Directions

1. Preheat oven to **425°F**. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
3. In a medium bowl, whisk together banana, melted butter, both sugars, eggs, and vanilla until well incorporated (some banana lumps are normal).
4. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and gently stir with a spatula until just combined — **do not overmix**.
5. Divide batter among liners (they'll be almost full).
6. Bake at 425°F for **5 minutes**, then reduce oven to **350°F** and bake another **15 minutes**, until centers are springy and tops are golden brown.
7. Cool in pan for a few minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Serve warm or cool completely.

## Tips

- **Don't overmix.** Stop when the last streaks of flour disappear — overmixing = dense, tough muffins.
- **Hot oven first.** The initial blast at 425°F creates the tall dome; then drop to 350°F so they don't burn.
- **Measure flour correctly.** Use a kitchen scale if possible, or fluff → spoon → level method.
- **Ripe bananas are key.** Peel should be mostly brown with very soft flesh.

## Variations

- **Banana nut:** Add ½–1 cup toasted walnuts or pecans
- **Whole wheat:** Swap flour for whole wheat (add 2 tbsp milk/water if using all WW)
- **Crumb topping:** Sprinkle streusel on top before baking
- **Chocolate chip:** Add up to 1 cup chocolate chips

## Storage & Freezing

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Room temp | Airtight container, up to 3 days |
| Fridge | Up to 1 week |
| Freeze | Wrap individually in plastic + foil, freezer bag, up to 3 months. Thaw on counter or overnight in fridge. |
