# Oatmeal Cookies

Soft, chewy oatmeal cookies — the ultimate classic cookie made from simple pantry staples. Perfectly spiced with cinnamon and loaded with old-fashioned oats.

**Source:** [Preppy Kitchen](https://preppykitchen.com/oatmeal-cookies/)

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| ---------- | -------- |
| All-Purpose Flour | 1½ cups (180g) |
| Baking Soda | ¾ teaspoon |
| Baking Powder | ¼ teaspoon |
| Salt | ½ teaspoon |
| Ground Cinnamon | 1 teaspoon |
| Unsalted Butter (room temp, softened) | 1 cup (226g) |
| Light Brown Sugar (packed) | 1 cup (220g) |
| Granulated Sugar | ½ cup (100g) |
| Eggs | 2 large, room temp |
| Vanilla Extract | 2 teaspoons |
| Old-Fashioned Rolled Oats | 2½ cups (280g) |
| Raisins or other mix-ins | 1 cup (160g) — optional |

**Yields:** ~30 cookies · **Prep:** 15 min · **Chill:** 1–72 hours · **Cook:** 12–14 min

## Directions

1. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon.
2. In a large mixing bowl (or stand mixer with paddle attachment), beat butter with both sugars on medium speed until light and fluffy (~3 minutes).
3. Reduce to low. Add eggs and vanilla, beat until well mixed (~1 minute). Add flour mixture and beat until almost combined.
4. Stir in oats and raisins (by hand — don't overmix!).
5. Scoop dough into 2-tablespoon balls onto a parchment-lined sheet. Cover and **chill for at least 1 hour** (up to 72 hours).
6. Preheat oven to **350°F**. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper. Place chilled dough balls ~2 inches apart.
7. Bake 12–14 minutes until edges are golden brown but centers still look slightly wet and shiny.
8. Cool on pans a few minutes, then transfer to wire rack. Let cool completely — this is when the chewy texture sets up.

## What Makes Them Perfectly Chewy

- **Both sugars.** Brown sugar = moisture + acidity for chew; granulated sugar = crispy edges. Together they're magic.
- **Old-fashioned rolled oats.** Don't substitute quick oats — they make dry, less-chewy cookies.
- **Chill the dough.** Up to 3 days in the fridge. Longer chill = chewier cookies.
- **Underbake slightly.** Pull them when centers still look wet — residual heat finishes the job as they cool.
- **Cool completely.** Vital for texture to set up properly.

## Variations (max 1½ cups total mix-ins)

| Variation | Details |
| --------- | ------- |
| Plain oatmeal | Skip mix-ins, add extra ½ cup oats |
| Oatmeal raisin | Use regular or golden raisins |
| Oatmeal cranberry | Dried cranberries |
| Other dried fruit | Cherries, chopped apricots |
| Nuts | Chopped pecans or walnuts for crunch |
| Chocolate chip | White, milk, or semi-sweet chips |

## Storage & Freezing

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Room temp | Airtight container, up to 4 days. Add a slice of bread or marshmallows to keep them soft. |
| Freeze (baked) | Parchment between layers in freezer-safe container, up to 3 months |
| Freeze (dough) | Scoop balls → flash freeze on sheet → transfer to bag/container, up to 3 months. Bake from frozen (+1–2 min bake time) |

## Pro Tips

- **Weigh your flour and oats.** Too much = dry cookies. A kitchen scale is the best way to get it right.
- **Room temp ingredients.** Butter and eggs incorporate better — if you forgot, soften butter in a few minutes.
- **Don't overmix.** Stir mix-ins by hand for tender cookies.
- **Use a cookie scoop (#40 = 2 tbsp).** Ensures even baking so all cookies finish at the same time.
