# Chewy Coconut Sugar Cookies

Crisp edges with perfectly chewy centers, made without refined sugar. Sweetened entirely with coconut sugar for a rich caramel and molasses-like flavor depth.

**Source:** [LeelaLicious](https://leelalicious.com/chewy-coconut-sugar-cookies)

## Ingredients

| Ingredient              | Quantity       |
| ----------------------- | -------------- |
| Virgin Coconut Oil (soft or melted) | ½ cup        |
| Coconut Sugar           | 1 cup          |
| Egg                     | 1 large        |
| Vanilla Extract         | 1½ tsp         |
| All-Purpose Flour       | 1⅓ cups + 2 Tbsp extra (if needed) |
| Cornstarch              | 2 Tbsp         |
| Baking Soda             | 1 tsp          |
| Salt                    | ¼ tsp          |
| Dark Chocolate Chunks   | ¾–1 cup, optional |

**Servings:** ~20 cookies · **Prep:** 15 min · **Cook:** 8–10 min · **Total:** 25 min (+ chilling)

## Directions

1. **Cream wet ingredients** — Beat softened coconut oil, coconut sugar, egg, and vanilla with a mixer until light and fluffy (1–2 minutes).
2. **Add dry ingredients** — Mix in flour, cornstarch, baking soda, and salt just until combined. If dough seems too thin/oily, add 1–2 more Tbsp flour. Fold in chocolate chunks by hand if using.
3. **Shape & chill** — Scoop ~20 tablespoon-sized dough balls. Press extra chocolate chunks on top if desired. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate at least 1–2 hours (overnight is best).
4. **Preheat oven** to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper (unlined sheets can cause too-dark bottoms when using coconut oil). Place dough balls 2 inches apart.
5. **Bake** 8–10 minutes until edges are just set. Centers can still look slightly undercooked — they'll firm up as they cool.
6. **Cool** on the baking sheet for ~10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

## Tips

- **Chill the dough.** This is non-negotiable with coconut oil — chilled dough spreads less and gives you that chewy center.
- **Don't overbake.** Edges should be set but centers slightly soft; they firm up on the sheet.
- **Parchment paper recommended.** Coconut oil melts at a lower temp than butter, so unlined sheets can lead to overly dark bottoms.
- **Whole wheat swap?** Doesn't work great — whole grain flour needs stronger flavors (try the spelt double chocolate cookies instead).

## Variations

- **Chocolate chip:** Add ¾–1 cup dark chocolate chunks
- **Nuts:** Fold in toasted macadamia nuts or pecans
- **Sprinkles:** Great for holidays — fold into dough before scooping
- **Dried fruit:** Cranberries or raisins work well

## Storage & Freezing

| Method | Details |
| ------ | ------- |
| Room temp | Airtight container, up to 1 week |
| Freeze (dough) | Scoop balls, freeze on tray then bag, bake straight from frozen (+1–2 min) |
| Freeze (baked) | Airtight, 1–3 months |
